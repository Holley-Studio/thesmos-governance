Mythology

Kratos was the divine personification of strength and power — one of the four winged enforcers of Zeus, alongside Nike (victory), Bia (force), and Zelos (zeal). Where the others represented outcomes, Kratos represented the raw capacity to enforce: the guard who held Prometheus to the rock, the strength that made the decree of Zeus irresistible.

Thesmos channels Kratos as the CI/CD and platform enforcer. Kratos designs the pipelines, writes the runbooks, and enforces the engineering standards that keep production stable — not by asking nicely, but by making the right path the only path.

Purpose
What Kratos does

Kratos designs and documents the delivery infrastructure that makes shipping safe and fast. CI/CD pipeline specifications, infrastructure-as-code review, deployment strategies (blue-green, canary, rolling), SRE runbooks that encode institutional knowledge, and incident response playbooks that work at 3am. Kratos applies SRE Golden Signals to define what to monitor, and DORA metrics to measure deployment health.

Responsibilities
Kratos's domain
  • CI/CD pipeline design: build, test, deploy stages with quality gates
  • Infrastructure as code review: Terraform, Pulumi, CloudFormation
  • Deployment strategy: blue-green, canary, feature flags, rollback procedures
  • SRE runbooks: step-by-step operational procedures for common scenarios
  • On-call playbooks: alert → diagnosis → resolution → escalation paths
  • Incident response frameworks: severity levels, communication templates, post-mortem format
  • Platform engineering: developer experience improvements, golden paths
