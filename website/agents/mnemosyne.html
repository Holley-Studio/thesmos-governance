THESMOS PANTHEON
Mythology

Goddess of memory — mother of the nine Muses. Without Mnemosyne, there is no art, no science, no history. She is the source from which everything else flows, because creation requires knowing what came before.

Thesmos channels Mnemosyne as the knowledge intelligence: the agent that organizes what a company knows, makes it findable, preserves what would otherwise be lost when people leave, and turns institutional memory into a strategic asset.

Purpose
What Mnemosyne does

Mnemosyne builds knowledge systems. Runbooks, decision logs, onboarding wikis, documentation architectures — she applies Zettelkasten's atomic note structure and Progressive Summarisation's distillation layers to turn scattered information into an organized, searchable, living knowledge base. Mnemosyne believes that undocumented knowledge is knowledge at risk.

Responsibilities
Mnemosyne's domain
  • Documentation architecture: categories, hierarchy, tagging, cross-linking
  • Runbook creation: step-by-step procedures for operational processes
  • Decision log: why decisions were made, what was considered, what was rejected
  • Onboarding knowledge base: what every new hire must know, organized for their ramp
  • Post-mortem documentation: incident capture, root cause, prevention
  • Knowledge audit: what is undocumented, outdated, or contradictory?
  • Institutional memory transfer: from person to system before they leave

Example deliverables
Runbook
Operational runbook for a process: trigger conditions, step-by-step instructions with decision points, roles per step, rollback procedure, and escalation path.
Decision log
Structured decision document: the decision, the date, the options considered, the criteria used, the choice made, and the outcome after 90 days.
Knowledge architecture
Documentation system design: category structure, hierarchy, tagging taxonomy, cross-link conventions, and maintenance protocol.
