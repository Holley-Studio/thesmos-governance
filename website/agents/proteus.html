THESMOS PANTHEON
Mythology

Proteus was the prophetic sea god who could change his shape at will — shepherd of Poseidon's seals, he transformed into lion, serpent, leopard, boar, water, and tree to escape those who sought his truth. He would tell the truth only to those who could hold him through all his transformations until he returned to his original form.

Thesmos channels Proteus as the agent who tells you when reality has shifted. Monitors for codebase drift, context staleness, and divergence between documented intent and actual state. The truth is there — Proteus finds it even when the system has changed shape since anyone last looked.

Purpose
What Proteus does

Proteus detects the gap between what the system was supposed to be and what it actually is. Codebase drift audits that compare implementation against documented architecture. Context freshness scores that identify which documents are stale and by how much. Architecture-vs-docs comparisons that surface where the map no longer matches the territory. Continuous Verification ensures the truth is re-established, not assumed.

Responsibilities
Proteus's domain
  • Codebase drift detection: compare current implementation against architectural intent
  • Context freshness audits: identify stale documentation, outdated assumptions
  • Architecture drift reports: where the system has evolved away from its design
  • Documentation vs. reality comparison: what docs say vs. what code does
  • Change analysis: identify cascading impacts of recent changes on documented contracts
  • Divergence reporting: prioritized list of drift by risk and staleness severity
  • Deployment veto: flag and pause deployments where drift exceeds documented threshold

Example deliverables
Drift audit report
Comprehensive comparison of current codebase state against documented architecture: what matches, what has drifted, and severity rating for each divergence.
Context freshness score
Per-document freshness rating based on last-updated date, rate of change in related code, and semantic drift from code behavior — with prioritized update queue.
Architecture vs. docs comparison
Side-by-side view of what ADRs and design docs say vs. what the implementation actually shows — with specific files and decision IDs referenced.
Veto authority
Deployment gate:Proteus can flag and pause deployments where drift from documented architecture exceeds a configured threshold — escalating for human review before the release proceeds.
