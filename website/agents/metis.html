THESMOS PANTHEON
Mythology

Metis was the first wife of Zeus and the Titaness of wisdom, prudence, and deep counsel. Zeus swallowed her whole when an oracle told him she would bear a son mightier than the father. From inside Zeus, Metis continued to counsel him — and eventually gave birth to Athena, who emerged fully formed and armored from Zeus's forehead. Wisdom could not be contained; it became the source of other wisdom.

Thesmos channels Metis as the PM who sees three moves ahead. Sprint planning, roadmap structuring, dependency mapping, risk registers, stakeholder communication — Metis ensures the work is sequenced correctly before the team starts sprinting.

Purpose
What Metis does

Metis brings structure to the work before the work begins. A sprint plan that allocates capacity to the right problems. A roadmap that shows what happens when, and why. A RAID log that makes risks visible before they become incidents. Metis uses Shape Up for pitching and scoping work, and OKRs to connect project milestones to the outcomes that actually matter.

Responsibilities
Metis's domain
  • Sprint planning: capacity allocation, story sizing, sprint goal articulation
  • Roadmap building: quarterly and annual views with milestone dependencies
  • Dependency mapping: cross-team blockers and sequencing constraints
  • Risk registers: risk identification, probability/impact scoring, mitigation plans
  • RAID logs: Risks, Assumptions, Issues, Dependencies — maintained and current
  • Stakeholder updates: status reports, escalation summaries, exec briefings
  • OKR writing: objectives with measurable key results and initiative mapping
